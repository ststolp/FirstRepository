I don't claim to be a photographer so I will do my best to analyze the following photos which I took in the Fall 2017 Semester.

Garden On Campus

Figure 1.0 A lovely afternoon creates great lighting on the garden at campus. A bow shaped glare is formed as the sun hits the lens just right.


I love this picture and I find it pleasing to the eye. The foreground, formed by a marble fence and bright bushes, constrasts well with the darker willow tree in the background. The rainbow-like bows give a dramatic touch.


River

Figure 1.1 A river creates mild crests in the water.


I took this picture with the intent to draw it later as a reference. While the photo may not follow any photography conventions, I hope you find joy in the simple things in life like a river.


Red leaves

Figure 1.2 A display of red leaves distract the photographer.

The variety of colors we see during the fall always fascinate me. I look and look and I can't get enough. I think that's why I've been drawn to draw leaves and all stuctures found in the plant kingdom. I feel a need to draw something in order to understand it better.

Bridge

Figure 1.3 A bridge outside of Rexburg.

I like how the tree on the left is serving as a frame for the side it sits on. I could do without the flood-control dam in the foreground. There's probably too much sky to follow the rule of thirds rule. Overall, there's not a whole lot going on to make it interesting.


Garden Gate

Figure 1.4 A Gate structure helps to beautify the garden on the BYU - Idaho campus.


In the garden at BYU - Idaho, there is a structure which appears to be a gate of some kind. I apologize for not taking the time to research the exact name. I especially enjoy the pots of plants in the foreground. Be cationed as the sun may hurt your eyes as I direct your attention to it. What would impove this photo is if the contrast of dark and light was toned down.
